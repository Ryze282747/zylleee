The "Neighbourhood" refers to an alternative rock and dark pop band formed in Newbury Park, California, in 2011 by vocalist Jesse Rutherford, guitarists Jeremy Freedman and Zach Abels, and bassist Mikey Margott. After a mysterious online launch, the band gained popularity with their EP I'm Sorry... and the single "Sweater Weather" in 2013, followed by the album I Love You.. Known for their black-and-white aesthetics and evolving sound, the band has continued to release music, including the album I Love You and subsequent EPs and albums, while maintaining a focus on dark, suburban themes.

The notable works of the band The Neighbourhood include the popular albums I Love You. (2013), Wiped Out! (2015), the self-titled The Neighbourhood (2018), and Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones (2020), along with the hit single "Sweater Weather" which reached #1 on the Billboard Alternative Songs chart. They are also known for their signature black-and-white aesthetic and have released several EPs, such as Hard and To Imagine, and the compilation EP Hard to Imagine The Neighbourhood Ever Changing.
